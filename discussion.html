Online Risks That Impact Children

Cyberbullying

Cyberbullying involves deliberate, repeated digital harassment. Unlike offline bullying, it occurs 24/7. Between 20% and 40% of adolescents report experiencing it, leading to depression and anxiety.

Online Predators

Online predators groom children by building trust. The IWF processed 255,588 URLs of abuse material in 2022, a 35% rise.

Inappropriate Content

Children often encounter inappropriate content. In fact, 52% of UK children aged 8-17 encountered disturbing content like hate speech or violence in 2020.

Privacy & Data Risks

Privacy is another major risk. Children's data is often collected without consent, and many lie about their age to access platforms.

Screen Addiction

Internet Gaming Disorder affects 1.6% to 8.5% of adolescents, disrupting sleep and physical activity.
