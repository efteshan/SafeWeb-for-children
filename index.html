ABOUT THIS RESEARCH

Why Children's Online Safety Matters

With one in three internet users globally being a child under 18, online safety is paramount. The COVID-19 pandemic further proved that internet access for children is a necessity for education and socialization, not a luxury.

This project focuses on the five primary risk categories: cyberbullying, grooming, inappropriate content, data privacy risks, and internet addiction. We take a balanced approach, emphasizing that while these risks are real, they are manageable with evidence-based interventions.

Restricting access entirely is counterproductive. Instead, our goal is equipping children and families with necessary digital skills, actionable tools, and the resilience to navigate the online world confidently.

  • 1 in 3 children encounter online sexual content (EU Kids Online)
  • Digital literacy education reduces vulnerability without limiting access
  • Parental controls work best combined with open family conversations
  • COPPA, GDPR, and the UK Online Safety Act protect children's data online
