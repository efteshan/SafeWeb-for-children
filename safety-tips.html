Online Safety Tips

Premium guidance for kids, parents, and schools. These tips are carefully designed to reduce risk while supporting confident learning and communication.

? Warning Signs Your Child May Be in Danger Online

  • They seem upset or secretive after being online.
  • They receive messages that make them feel uncomfortable or scared.
  • They suddenly change privacy settings without explanation.
  • They delete chats, pictures, or accounts without telling you.
  • They receive requests from unfamiliar accounts or strangers.
  • You notice a sharp drop in mood, sleep, or school focus.

TIPS FOR CHILDREN

Stay in Control of Your Online Life

  1. Know What Feels Wrong If something makes you feel unsafe, stop and tell a trusted adult right away.
  2. Check Links Before You Click Donĺt open messages or downloads from people you donĺt know.
  3. Protect Your Personal Info Never share your address, passwords, school details, or location.
  4. Be Kind and Think First Posts and messages can last a long time, so always think about how your words affect others.
  5. Use Privacy Settings Keep your profiles limited and review who can see and contact you.
  6. Take Breaks When Needed Manage screen time and ask for help if you feel stuck scrolling.

TIPS FOR PARENTS

Set Boundaries That Protect

  1. Talk Before Problems Happen Use regular check-ins so children feel safe reporting concerns without fear.
  2. Configure Privacy and Filters Use device settings and trusted safety tools to limit exposure.
  3. Review Activity Patterns Look for sudden behavior changes, and then make sure to ask calm, supportive questions.
  4. Teach ôBlock and Reportö Make sure children know exactly what to do when messages feel unsafe.
  5. Balance Screen Time Create schedules and encourage offline activities that improve sleep and focus.
  6. Keep Evidence Screenshot harmful content and save links when reporting to schools or services.

TIPS FOR SCHOOLS

Build Safer Learning Communities

  1. Teach Digital Literacy Continuously Include safety lessons in class and reinforce them through real scenarios.
  2. Support Reporting Paths Ensure students know how to report quickly and feel protected during follow-up.
  3. Promote Respectful Norms Encourage empathy, reduce stigma, and address bullying behavior early.
  4. Coordinate With Families Share guidance, align policies, and respond consistently across devices and platforms.
