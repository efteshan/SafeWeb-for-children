Online Safety Tips

Premium guidance for kids, parents, and schools. These tips are carefully designed to reduce risk while supporting confident learning and communication.

TIPS FOR CHILDREN

Stay in Control of Your Online Life

  1. Protect Your Privacy Never share your address, passwords, or personal information. Be kind and think critically about what you post.
  2. Manage Screen Time Always take breaks when needed to manage screen time effectively. Use privacy settings to limit who can contact you.
  3. Know What Feels Wrong If something makes you feel unsafe, stop and tell a trusted adult right away.
  4. Check Links Before You Click Don't open messages or downloads from people you don't know.
  5. Protect Your Personal Info Never share your address, passwords, school details, or location.
  6. Be Kind and Think First Posts and messages can last a long time, so always think about how your words affect others.
  7. Use Privacy Settings Keep your profiles limited and review who can see and contact you.
  8. Take Breaks When Needed Manage screen time and ask for help if you feel stuck scrolling.

TIPS FOR PARENTS

Set Boundaries That Protect

  1. Communicate and Monitor Maintain open conversations, use content filtering, understand COPPA rights, and actively monitor screen time.
  2. Watch for Grooming Grooming often begins with trust-building, secrecy, and desensitization. Always be vigilant for these signs.
  3. Encourage Openness Talk before problems happen to ensure children feel safe asking for help without fear of losing access.
  4. Talk Before Problems Happen Use regular check-ins so children feel safe reporting concerns without fear.
  5. Configure Privacy and Filters Use device settings and trusted safety tools to limit exposure.
  6. Review Activity Patterns Look for sudden behavior changes, and then make sure to ask calm, supportive questions.
  7. Teach Block and Report Make sure children know exactly what to do when messages feel unsafe.
  8. Balance Screen Time Create schedules and encourage offline activities that improve sleep and focus.
  9. Keep Evidence Screenshot harmful content and save links when reporting to schools or services.

TIPS FOR SCHOOLS

Build Safer Learning Communities

  1. Implement Literacy Curricula Schools should implement evidence-based digital literacy curricula from the primary level to teach critical evaluation.
  2. Teach Digital Literacy Continuously Include safety lessons in class and reinforce them through real scenarios.
  3. Support Reporting Paths Ensure students know how to report quickly and feel protected during follow-up.
  4. Promote Respectful Norms Encourage empathy, reduce stigma, and address bullying behavior early.
  5. Coordinate With Families Share guidance, align policies, and respond consistently across devices and platforms.
