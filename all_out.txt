            <article class="content-card" aria-label="What is the World Wide Web section">
              <h2>What is the World Wide Web</h2>
              <p>
                The World Wide Web was developed to make information easily shareable across computers. Proposed by Tim
                Berners-Lee, it grew rapidly into a global network of websites, services, and connected experiences.
              </p>
              <p>
                Today, the web powers learning, communication, entertainment, and community. This often happens directly from the devices that
                children use every day.
              </p>
            </article>

            <article class="content-card" aria-label="The rise of children online section">
              <h2>The Rise of Children Online</h2>
              <p>
                Access to the internet is increasingly common at younger ages. Alongside this growth comes changing
                patterns of online behavior, including higher exposure to harmful content and peer-to-peer conflict.
              </p>
              <p>
                Screen time data also suggests that many children spend more hours online than their adults realize,
                which increases the need for active guidance and age-appropriate safeguards.
              </p>
            </article>

            <article class="content-card" aria-label="Digital generation section">
              <h2>The Digital Generation</h2>
              <p>
                Todays children are often born connected. They learn platforms quickly and explore social spaces with
                little experience of privacy risks, manipulation tactics, and online permanence.
              </p>
              <p>
                This creates a different challenge than earlier eras: safety is not only about avoiding danger, but also
                about building judgment and resilience.
              </p>
            </article>

            <article class="content-card" aria-label="Why this matters section" style="grid-column: 1 / -1;  margin-top: 1rem; text-align: left;">
              <h2 style="margin-bottom: 0.5rem; text-align: left;">Why This Matters</h2>
              <p>
                Internet safety affects mental health, education, and social development. Harmful interactions can shape
                how children feel about themselves, how they concentrate, and how they trust others.
              </p>
              <p>
                When children understand boundaries and adults use effective controls, the web becomes a place where curiosity
                can thrive safely.
              </p>
            </article>

        </div>

              <h3>COPPA (USA)</h3>
              <p>
                The Children's Online Privacy Protection Act restricts how websites collect data from children under 13 and
                requires parental consent for certain data practices.
              </p>
              <ul class="checklist" aria-label="COPPA key points">
                <li><span class="check" aria-hidden="true"></span>Limits online data collection from minors</li>
                <li><span class="check" aria-hidden="true"></span>Requires parental consent where needed</li>
                <li><span class="check" aria-hidden="true"></span>Improves transparency for families</li>
              </ul>
            </article>

            <article class="card" aria-label="GDPR Children regulation card">
              <h3>GDPR Children (EU)</h3>
              <p>
                GDPR principles influence how organizations process children's personal data, emphasizing lawful basis, data
                minimization, and child-sensitive protections.
              </p>
              <ul class="checklist" aria-label="GDPR Children key points">
                <li><span class="check" aria-hidden="true"></span>Prioritizes privacy-by-design safeguards</li>
                <li><span class="check" aria-hidden="true"></span>Supports rights to access and deletion</li>
                <li><span class="check" aria-hidden="true"></span>Promotes clearer consent standards</li>
